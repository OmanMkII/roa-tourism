The Museum of Alternate History

The Museum of Alternate History showcases some of the most incredible discoveries to date, with many more being uncovered as scientists excavate the facilities discovered and further investigate their findings. With the newfound ability to travel time, historians have seized the opportunity to observe their studies first hand and take samples in pristine conditions.

An Impossible Future Exhibit

Although many historians predict that our current civilisations will follow in the footsteps of those who stumbled, this exhibit explores some of the radically alternate histories that have been discovered.

Historians continue to look into exactly how these alternate histories began; some were caused by systemic differences that ended worlds, such as the AI revolution of 2093; others were seemingly insignificant events that only caused change years - sometimes decades - later.

Millennia Of War Exhibit

A frequent truth of history is that factions often clash: at times this can lead to war. Over the last few millennia, the method of war has changed radically with weapons progressing from archaic sword and bow, to the simple rifle, and now to plasma weaponry.

Once complete, this exhibit plans to explore the innovations of weaponry throughout our history, with special mention to some of the less successful forms of pathogenic warfare attempted in the early 21st century.

On The Shoulder Of Giants Exhibit

Much of the current generation of technology has been built on the basis of ideas and theories proposed by those before us, and much like their knowledge we have inherited their physical attributes. Over time, humans have evolved from minor primate species to major powers in the galactic empire, which will likely accelerate with the relatively recent creation of fully independent Artificial Intelligence.

This exhibit plans to explore the influence of humans throughout the galaxy both before and after the collapse of the original industrial revolution. Much of our current technology is based on designs from this era and it remains a prominent part of our history and culture today.
